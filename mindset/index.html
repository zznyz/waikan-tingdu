The Power of 'Yet'
0:001:06
▶ 念到哪句，哪句高亮
英 文 原 文 · 听 读
Why do some students give up the moment they fail, while others keep trying? Stanford psychologist Carol Dweck spent years on this question. Her answer comes down to one idea: how we think about our own ability能力. People hold one of two mindsets. Those with a "fixed" mindset believe talent is something you are born with. Those with a "growth" mindset believe they can develop发展 their skills through effort努力, the right strategy策略, and asking for help. Facing a challenge挑战, they see a chance to grow. The difference shows up in one small word: "yet. A student who thinks "I can't do this" feels defeated. One who says "I can't do this yet" turns failure into a point the learner can use to improve提高. How we praise表扬 children matters too. Praising a child's effort — not her cleverness — teaches her to keep going when the work gets tough. The brain is like a muscle that grows stronger with use. When students believe their potential潜力 can expand, both effort and grades rise. Ability is not a fate we are handed — it is something we build.
参 考 译 文
为什么有的学生一失败就放弃，有的却还在坚持？斯坦福心理学家卡罗尔·德韦克花了多年研究这个问题。答案归结为一个观念：我们如何看待自己的能力。人们往往持两种思维之一。"固定型"思维的人相信天赋与生俱来；"成长型"思维的人相信，自己能通过努力、正确的策略和向他人求助来发展技能。面对挑战，他们看到的是成长的机会。这种差异体现在一个小词上——"yet"（还没）：说"我还做不到"的学生，把失败变成了可以用来提高自己的一个点。我们表扬孩子的方式同样重要：夸努力而非夸聪明，才能教会孩子在变难时坚持下去。大脑更像一块越用越强的肌肉。当学生相信潜力可以拓展，努力和成绩都会随之提升。原来，能力不是命运，而是我们亲手建造的东西。
